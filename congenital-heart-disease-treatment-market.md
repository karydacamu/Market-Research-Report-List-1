<p><p>The congenital heart disease treatment market comprises two primary types: medication and surgical therapy. Medication involves the use of drugs to manage symptoms, improve heart function, and prevent complications. Common medications include diuretics, beta-blockers, and anticoagulants. Surgical therapy encompasses various procedures ranging from minimally invasive techniques to open-heart surgeries aimed at correcting structural heart defects. These interventions can involve repairing or replacing heart valves, closing holes between heart chambers, or redirecting blood flow, ultimately improving patients' quality of life and outcomes.</p></p>
