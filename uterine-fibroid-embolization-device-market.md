<p><p>The Uterine Fibroid Embolization Device market comprises various materials used to block blood flow to fibroids, promoting their shrinkage. Gelatin sponge is a biodegradable option that promotes clotting. Polyvinyl Alcohol (PVA) particles are non-biodegradable, offering durable occlusion. Trisacryl Gelatin Microspheres (TAGM) provide a controlled release and are less thrombogenic. Polymethyl Methacrylate (PMMA) microspheres are biocompatible and effective in targeting fibroid vascularity. Each type serves specific needs within the embolization procedure, impacting treatment efficacy and patient outcomes.</p></p>
