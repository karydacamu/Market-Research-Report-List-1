<p><p>The all-solid-state lithium battery market consists of two primary types: polymer-based all-solid-state batteries and all-solid-state batteries with inorganic solid electrolytes. Polymer-based batteries utilize solid polymer electrolytes to enhance flexibility and safety, making them suitable for various applications. In contrast, batteries with inorganic solid electrolytes employ more rigid materials, offering higher ionic conductivity and energy density. Both types aim to improve battery performance, safety, and longevity, addressing the limitations of conventional lithium-ion batteries while enabling advancements in energy storage technology.</p></p>
