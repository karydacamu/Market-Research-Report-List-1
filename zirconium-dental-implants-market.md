<p><p>Zirconium dental implants primarily consist of zirconium oxide, but there are variations that incorporate cobalt compounds. Cobalt oxide is sometimes used for its biocompatibility and strength, making it suitable for high-stress applications. Cobalt alloys, which combine cobalt with other metals, enhance mechanical properties and corrosion resistance, offering durability in dental applications. Other materials may include titanium composites or alternative ceramics, emphasizing aesthetic appeal and compatibility with natural bone. The choice of material affects the implant's performance, longevity, and patient satisfaction.</p></p>
